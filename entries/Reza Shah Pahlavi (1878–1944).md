## Early Life
- Born: **March 15, 1878**, in Alasht, Mazandaran, Iran.  
- Originally named **Reza Khan**.  
- Rose from humble origins to become a military officer in the Persian Cossack Brigade.  

## Rise to Power
- Led a coup in **1921**, becoming Minister of War and later Prime Minister.  
- In **1925**, deposed the Qajar dynasty and was crowned Shah, founding the **Pahlavi dynasty**.  

## Reign (1925–1941)
- Focused on **modernization and centralization**:  
  - Built railways, roads, and modern schools.  
  - Reorganized the army and bureaucracy.  
  - Promoted secularism, reduced clerical influence.  
  - Changed Persia’s official name to **Iran (1935)**.  
- Encouraged Westernization, including dress codes and banning the veil in public.  

## Challenges
- His authoritarian style limited political freedoms.  
- Economic reforms often favored elites over rural populations.  
- Growing tensions with Britain and the Soviet Union during WWII.  

## Abdication and Death
- In **1941**, Allied forces invaded Iran to secure oil and supply routes.  
- Reza Shah was forced to abdicate in favor of his son, **Mohammad Reza Shah Pahlavi**.  
- Exiled to South Africa, where he died on **July 26, 1944**.  

## Legacy
- Remembered as the **“Father of Modern Iran”** for his modernization efforts.  
- Criticized for authoritarianism and lack of democratic reforms.  
- His dynasty lasted until the **1979 Islamic Revolution**, which ended monarchy in Iran.
