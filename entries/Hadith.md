# Hadith

Hadith (حديث) refers to the recorded sayings, actions, and approvals of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ). Alongside the Qur’an, Hadith forms the foundation of Islamic law (Sharia), theology, and daily practice.

## Structure of a Hadith

1. **Isnad (Chain of Transmission)** – The list of narrators who passed down the report.  
2. **Matn (Text)** – The actual content of the saying or action.  

## Classification

- **Sahih (Authentic)** – Strong chain of transmission, reliable narrators.  
- **Hasan (Good)** – Acceptable but slightly weaker chain.  
- **Da‘if (Weak)** – Weak chain or questionable narrators.  
- **Mawdu‘ (Fabricated)** – Invented reports, rejected by scholars.  

## Major Collections

- **Sahih al‑Bukhari**  
- **Sahih Muslim**  
- **Sunan Abu Dawud**  
- **Jami‘ at‑Tirmidhi**  
- **Sunan an‑Nasa’i**  
- **Sunan Ibn Majah**  
- **Muwatta Malik**  

## Importance in Islam

* Explains and contextualizes the Qur’an.  
* Provides guidance on worship, ethics, and social conduct.  
* Forms the basis of Islamic jurisprudence (Fiqh).  
* Preserves the legacy and teachings of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ).  

## Example Hadith

> “Actions are judged by intentions, and every man shall have only that which he intended.”  
(*Sahih al‑Bukhari, Sahih Muslim*)

*Hadith remain indispensable for Islamic scholarship and practice, shaping law, spirituality, and daily life for Muslims worldwide.*
