## Early Life
- Born: **22 April 1870**, Simbirsk (now Ulyanovsk), Russia.  
- Birth name: **Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov**.  
- Studied law, influenced by Marxist ideas after his brother was executed for plotting against the Tsar.  

## Revolutionary Career
- Joined the **Russian Social Democratic Labour Party (RSDLP)**.  
- Split the party into **Bolsheviks** (radical) and **Mensheviks** (moderate) in 1903.  
- Exiled multiple times, lived in Europe, wrote extensively on Marxism and revolution.  

## October Revolution (1917)
- Returned to Russia during World War I.  
- Led the **Bolsheviks** in overthrowing the Provisional Government in October 1917.  
- Established the **Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic (RSFSR)**.

## Death
- Suffered strokes beginning in 1922.  
- Died: **21 January 1924**, Gorki, near Moscow.  
- His body was embalmed and placed in the **Lenin Mausoleum** in Red Square, Moscow.  

## Legacy
- Regarded as the **architect of Soviet communism**.  
- His ideas, known as **Leninism**, influenced global communist movements.  
- Controversial figure: admired as a revolutionary leader, criticized for authoritarian methods.
