## Definition
The **Quadrilateral Security Dialogue (QUAD)** is an intergovernmental security forum of four major Indo‑Pacific democracies: **Australia, India, Japan, and the United States**. It promotes cooperation on security, technology, health, and infrastructure.

## Origins
- First proposed by **Japanese PM Shinzo Abe in 2007**.  
- Initially dissolved in 2008 due to diplomatic pressures.  
- Revived in **2017**, gaining momentum amid rising regional tensions.  

## Objectives
- Ensure a **free and open Indo‑Pacific**.  
- Strengthen **maritime security** and freedom of navigation.  
- Collaborate on **climate change, technology, cyber security, health, and infrastructure**.  
- Counterbalance regional challenges, including China’s growing influence.  

## Key Activities
- **Annual QUAD Leaders’ Summits**.  
- Joint military exercises (e.g., **Malabar naval exercise**).  
- Working groups in areas like **climate, critical technologies, cyber, health security, infrastructure, and space**.  

## Importance
- Represents **four major democracies** with strong economies and militaries.  
- Covers nearly **35% of global GDP** and a significant share of world trade.  
- Acts as a stabilizing force in the Indo‑Pacific.  

---

*QUAD is not a formal military alliance like NATO, but rather a flexible partnership aimed at shaping a secure and prosperous Indo‑Pacific region.*
