## Early Life
- Born: **August 13, 1926**, in Birán, Cuba.  
- Studied law at the University of Havana, where he became politically active.  

## Rise to Power
- Led the **Cuban Revolution** against dictator Fulgencio Batista.  
- In **1959**, Batista fled Cuba, and Castro became Prime Minister.  
- Later served as **President of Cuba (1976–2008)** and **First Secretary of the Communist Party (1965–2011)**.  

## Leadership
- Established Cuba as a **one‑party socialist state** aligned with the Soviet Union.  
- Implemented sweeping reforms in **healthcare, education, and land redistribution**.  
- Criticized for suppressing dissent, restricting freedoms, and creating economic hardships.  

## Relations with the U.S.
- Oversaw the **Bay of Pigs invasion (1961)** and the **Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)**.  
- U.S. imposed a trade embargo that lasted decades.  
- Survived **hundreds of assassination attempts** reportedly backed by the CIA.  

## Legacy
- Praised for achievements in literacy, healthcare, and racial equality.  
- Criticized for authoritarian rule and economic stagnation.  
- Stepped down in **2008**, succeeded by his brother **Raúl Castro**.  
- Died: **November 25, 2016**, in Havana, Cuba.  

---

*Fidel Castro remains a symbol of revolutionary defiance, admired by some for social reforms and condemned by others for authoritarianism.*
