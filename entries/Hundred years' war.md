# Hundred Years’ War (1337–1453)

## Causes
- Dispute over succession to the **French crown**: English King **Edward III** claimed the throne through his mother, a French princess.  
- Rivalry over **territories in France**, especially Gascony.  
- Economic tensions: control of trade routes and wool markets.  

## Major Phases
1. **Edwardian War (1337–1360)**  
   - English victories at **Crécy (1346)** and **Poitiers (1356)**.  
   - Treaty of Brétigny (1360) gave England large territories.  

2. **Caroline War (1369–1389)**  
   - French King **Charles V** regained much land.  
   - England weakened by internal strife.  

3. **Lancastrian War (1415–1453)**  
   - English King **Henry V** won at **Agincourt (1415)**.  
   - Treaty of Troyes (1420) recognized Henry V as heir to French throne.  
   - Rise of **Joan of Arc (1429)**, who inspired French victories at Orléans and Patay.  
   - French resurgence under Charles VII led to final expulsion of English forces.  

## Key Figures
- **Edward III of England** – initiated the war.  
- **Henry V of England** – victor at Agincourt.  
- **Joan of Arc** – French heroine who turned the tide.  
- **Charles VII of France** – restored French power.  

## Outcome
- **French victory** in 1453 at the Battle of Castillon.  
- England lost all continental possessions except **Calais**.  
- Strengthened French monarchy and national identity.  
- England entered internal turmoil (Wars of the Roses).  

## Legacy
- Shifted warfare: decline of feudal knights, rise of professional armies and gunpowder weapons.  
- Boosted French nationalism.  
- Marked the end of medieval Europe and transition toward the Renaissance.
