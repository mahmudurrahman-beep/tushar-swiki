## Origins
- Established: Around **882 CE** by Prince **Oleg of Novgorod**, who moved the capital to Kyiv.  
- Founders: The **Rus’ people**, likely of Scandinavian (Varangian) origin.  
- Territory: Covered parts of modern **Ukraine, Belarus, and Russia**.  

## Government & Society
- Rulers: The **Rurikid dynasty** (Oleg, Igor, Olga, Sviatoslav, Vladimir the Great, Yaroslav the Wise).  
- Political system: Monarchy, supported by assemblies called **veche**.  
- Languages: **Old East Slavic** and **Old Church Slavonic** (liturgical).  
- Religion:  
  - Paganism until the **Christianization of Rus’ in 988** under **Vladimir the Great**.  
  - Adopted **Eastern Orthodox Christianity**, linking Rus’ to Byzantium.  

## Golden Age
- Peak: **10th–11th centuries**, especially under **Yaroslav the Wise (1019–1054)**.  
- Achievements:  
  - Codified laws (*Russkaya Pravda*).  
  - Built churches like **Saint Sophia Cathedral in Kyiv**.  
  - Expanded trade routes between Scandinavia, Byzantium, and the Islamic world.  

## Decline
- Began after Yaroslav’s death in **1054**, due to succession disputes.  
- Fragmentation into regional principalities (Novgorod, Vladimir-Suzdal, Galicia-Volhynia).  
- Weakened by internal strife and invasions.  
- Ended with the **Mongol invasion (1240)**, when Kyiv was destroyed.  

## Legacy
- Considered the **cultural ancestor** of Russia, Ukraine, and Belarus.  
- Spread Orthodox Christianity and Slavic literacy.  
- Influenced law, art, and political traditions in Eastern Europe.
