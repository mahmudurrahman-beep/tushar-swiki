## Early Life
- Born: **5 June 1723 (baptised 16 June)**, Kirkcaldy, Scotland.  
- Education: Studied at the **University of Glasgow** and **Balliol College, Oxford**.  
- Became a professor of logic and moral philosophy at the University of Glasgow.  

## Major Works
- **The Theory of Moral Sentiments (1759):**  
  - Explored ethics, sympathy, and human behavior.  
  - Argued that moral judgments arise from empathy and social interaction.  
- **An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations (1776):**  
  - Considered the first modern work of economics.  
  - Advocated free markets, competition, and limited government intervention.  
  - Introduced the concept of the **“invisible hand”**, where individuals pursuing self-interest unintentionally benefit society.  

## Key Ideas
- **Division of Labour:** Specialization increases productivity.  
- **Free Markets:** Prices and trade should be guided by supply and demand.  
- **Absolute Advantage:** Nations should trade based on efficiency in production.  
- **Role of Government:** Should provide defense, justice, and public works, but avoid excessive interference in markets.  

## Later Life
- Worked as a commissioner of customs in Edinburgh.  
- Died: **17 July 1790**, Edinburgh, Scotland.  
- Buried in **Canongate Kirkyard**.  

## Legacy
- Known as the **“Father of Economics”**.  
- His ideas shaped **classical economics** and influenced later thinkers like David Ricardo and John Stuart Mill.  
- Still central to debates on capitalism, free trade, and government regulation.
