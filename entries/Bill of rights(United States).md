## Origins
- Proposed by the **1st U.S. Congress** in 1789.  
- Ratified by the states in **1791**.  
- Influenced by earlier documents: **Magna Carta (1215)**, **English Bill of Rights (1689)**, and **Virginia Declaration of Rights (1776)**.  
- Drafted primarily by **James Madison** to protect individual liberties and limit government power.  

## Contents (First 10 Amendments)
1. **Freedom of Speech, Religion, Press, Assembly, Petition**  
2. **Right to Bear Arms**  
3. **Quartering of Soldiers** (protection against forced housing of troops)  
4. **Protection against Unreasonable Searches and Seizures**  
5. **Due Process, Double Jeopardy, Self-Incrimination**  
6. **Right to a Fair and Speedy Trial**  
7. **Right to Trial by Jury in Civil Cases**  
8. **Protection against Cruel and Unusual Punishment**  
9. **Rights Retained by the People** (not limited to those listed)  
10. **Powers Reserved to the States and the People**  

## Importance
- Established **individual freedoms** as constitutional guarantees.  
- Limited federal government power.  
- Became a model for rights protections worldwide.  
- Continues to shape debates on civil liberties, privacy, and government authority.  

## Legacy
The Bill of Rights remains a cornerstone of **American democracy**, ensuring that government power is balanced by the protection of personal freedoms.
