## Definition
The **IAEA** is the world’s center for cooperation in the nuclear field. It promotes safe, secure, and peaceful nuclear technologies while monitoring compliance with treaties like the **Nuclear Non‑Proliferation Treaty (NPT)**.

## Origins
- Established: **July 29, 1957**.  
- Motto: **“Atoms for Peace and Development.”**  
- Created in response to growing concerns about nuclear weapons and the need for peaceful nuclear applications.  

## Headquarters & Structure
- Headquarters: **Vienna, Austria**.  
- Regional offices: Geneva, New York, Tokyo, Toronto, Monaco, Seibersdorf, Trieste.  
- Current Director General: **Rafael Grossi** (since 2019).  
- Staff: ~2,500 professionals.  

## Functions
- **Safeguards & Verification** – Ensures nuclear materials are not diverted to weapons.  
- **Safety & Security** – Sets international standards for nuclear safety.  
- **Science & Technology** – Promotes nuclear applications in medicine, agriculture, and energy.  
- **Emergency Response** – Assists in nuclear accidents (e.g., Fukushima, Chernobyl).  

## Membership
- **180+ member states**.  
- Works closely with the **United Nations** and other international organizations.  

## Importance
- Monitors nuclear programs worldwide (e.g., Iran, North Korea).  
- Provides technical assistance for peaceful nuclear energy.  
- Plays a vital role in global security and sustainable development.
