## Early Life
- Born: **August 4, 1961**, Honolulu, Hawaii.  
- Parents: Barack Obama Sr. (Kenyan) and Ann Dunham (American).  
- Education: Columbia University (BA, 1983), Harvard Law School (JD, 1991).  
- Became the first Black president of the **Harvard Law Review**.  

## Political Career
- Illinois State Senator (1997–2004).  
- U.S. Senator from Illinois (2005–2008).  
- Elected **President of the United States in 2008**, re‑elected in 2012.  

## Presidency (2009–2017)
- Major achievements:  
  - **Affordable Care Act (Obamacare)** – expanded healthcare access.  
  - **Economic Recovery Act (2009)** – response to the financial crisis.  
  - Ordered the mission that killed **Osama bin Laden (2011)**.  
  - Promoted clean energy and climate change initiatives.  
  - Supported marriage equality and civil rights expansion.  
- Foreign policy: Ended combat operations in Iraq, pivot to Asia, nuclear deal with Iran.  

## Legacy
- First African American president.  
- Nobel Peace Prize (2009).  
- Author of books: *Dreams from My Father*, *The Audacity of Hope*, *A Promised Land*.  
- Continues to influence global discussions on democracy, leadership, and equality.  

## Personal Life
- Married **Michelle Obama** in 1992.  
- Two daughters: **Malia** and **Sasha**.
