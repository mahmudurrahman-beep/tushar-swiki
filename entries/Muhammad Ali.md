## Early Life
- Born: **January 17, 1942**, Louisville, Kentucky.  
- Began boxing at age 12 after his bike was stolen.  
- Won a **gold medal at the 1960 Rome Olympics** in light heavyweight boxing.  

## Boxing Career
- Professional debut: **1960**.  
- Won the **world heavyweight title in 1964** by defeating Sonny Liston.  
- Famous bouts:  
  - *Fight of the Century* vs. Joe Frazier (1971).  
  - *Rumble in the Jungle* vs. George Foreman (1974).  
  - *Thrilla in Manila* vs. Joe Frazier (1975).  
- Retired in **1981** with a record of **56 wins (37 by KO) and 5 losses**.  

## Activism & Legacy
- Converted to Islam in 1964, changing his name to Muhammad Ali.  
- Refused to fight in the **Vietnam War (1967)**, citing religious beliefs and opposition to the conflict.  
- Stripped of his title but later reinstated.  
- Advocated for civil rights, humanitarian causes, and global peace.  

## Later Life
- Diagnosed with **Parkinson’s disease in 1984**.  
- Continued public appearances, including lighting the Olympic torch in Atlanta (1996).  
- Died: **June 3, 2016**, Scottsdale, Arizona, aged 74.  

## Legacy
- Nicknamed **“The Greatest.”**  
- Remembered for his boxing skill, wit, and courage outside the ring.  
- Inspired generations as both an athlete and activist.
