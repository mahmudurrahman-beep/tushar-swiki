## Early Life
- Born: **February 22, 1732**, Westmoreland County, Virginia.  
- Worked as a surveyor before joining the military.  
- Gained early experience in the **French and Indian War (1754–1763)**.  

## Revolutionary War
- Appointed **Commander-in-Chief of the Continental Army (1775)**.  
- Led American forces to victory against Britain, including decisive moments at **Trenton (1776)** and **Yorktown (1781)**.  
- Resigned his commission in 1783, symbolizing civilian control of the military.  

## Presidency (1789–1797)
- Unanimously elected as the **first U.S. President**.  
- Established key precedents:  
  - Two-term limit (voluntarily stepped down after 8 years).  
  - Formation of a Cabinet.  
  - Neutrality in foreign affairs.  
- Oversaw the **Bill of Rights (1791)** and the creation of a stable federal government.  

## Legacy
- Known as the *“Father of His Country.”*  
- His image appears on the **U.S. dollar bill** and the **quarter coin**.  
- Remembered for his Farewell Address, warning against political parties and foreign entanglements.  
- Died: **December 14, 1799**, at Mount Vernon, Virginia.
