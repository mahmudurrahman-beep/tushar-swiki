Accounting is the systematic process of recording, classifying, summarizing, and interpreting financial transactions of an organization. It provides essential information for decision‑making, control, and reporting.

## Historical Development

1. **Ancient Practices** – Early record‑keeping in Mesopotamia, Egypt, and Rome.  
2. **Double‑Entry System (15th Century)** – Introduced by Luca Pacioli in Italy, forming the foundation of modern accounting.  
3. **Industrial Revolution** – Expansion of corporate accounting and auditing.  
4. **20th Century** – Standardization through Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS).  

## Major Branches

- **Financial Accounting** – Preparation of financial statements for external users.  
- **Managerial Accounting** – Internal reporting for planning and control.  
- **Cost Accounting** – Analysis of production costs and efficiency.  
- **Auditing** – Independent examination of financial records.  
- **Tax Accounting** – Compliance with tax laws and regulations.  

## Fundamental Concepts

- **Assets, Liabilities, Equity** – Basic elements of the balance sheet.  
- **Revenue and Expenses** – Components of the income statement.  
- **Accrual Principle** – Transactions recorded when they occur, not when cash is exchanged.  
- **Matching Principle** – Expenses matched with related revenues.  
- **Going Concern** – Assumption that the entity will continue operating.  

## Key Statements

1. **Balance Sheet** – Shows financial position at a specific date.  
2. **Income Statement** – Reports performance over a period.  
3. **Cash Flow Statement** – Tracks inflows and outflows of cash.  
4. **Statement of Changes in Equity** – Explains movements in owners’ equity.  

## Importance

* Provides transparency and accountability.  
* Supports investment and lending decisions.  
* Ensures compliance with laws and regulations.  
* Helps management in planning and control.  

## Key Figures

- **Luca Pacioli (1447–1517)** – Father of modern accounting.  
- **Modern Standard‑Setters** – International Accounting Standards Board (IASB), Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB).  

---

*Accounting is often called the "language of business," enabling organizations to communicate financial information clearly and reliably.*
