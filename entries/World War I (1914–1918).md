# World War I (1914–1918)

**World War I**, also known as the **Great War**, was a global conflict fought mainly in Europe from **1914 to 1918**. It involved most of the world’s great powers, divided into two alliances: the **Allies** (including Britain, France, Russia, later the United States) and the **Central Powers** (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire).

## Causes
- Assassination of **Archduke Franz Ferdinand** in Sarajevo (1914).
- Rising nationalism, militarism, and complex alliances.
- Competition for colonies and global influence.

## Major Events
- Trench warfare on the Western Front.
- Battles such as the **Somme**, **Verdun**, and **Gallipoli**.
- Use of new technologies: machine guns, poison gas, tanks, aircraft.

## Outcome
- Ended with the **Armistice of November 11, 1918**.
- Treaty of **Versailles (1919)** imposed heavy reparations on Germany.
- Collapse of empires: Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian.
- Estimated **16 million deaths** and massive destruction.

## Significance
- Redrew the map of Europe and the Middle East.
- Set the stage for **World War II** due to unresolved tensions.
- Inspired advances in medicine, technology, and international diplomacy.
