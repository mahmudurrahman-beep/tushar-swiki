## Definition
**Prophet Muhammad ibn Abdullah (peace be upon him)** was the founder of Islam and is regarded by Muslims as the **last prophet of God**. He is considered the messenger through whom the Qur’an was revealed, guiding humanity toward monotheism and moral conduct.

## Early Life
- Born: **570 CE** in **Mecca**, known as the "Year of the Elephant."  
- Orphaned at a young age; raised by his grandfather Abdul Muttalib and later his uncle Abu Talib.  
- Worked as a merchant, earning the title **Al‑Amin** (the trustworthy).  
- Married **Khadijah**, a wealthy merchant widow, at age 25.  

## Prophethood
- At age 40, received his first revelation in the **Cave of Hira** through the Angel Jibreel (Gabriel).  
- Preached **Tawhid** (oneness of God), social justice, and moral reform.  
- Faced persecution in Mecca; followers endured hardship.  
- Migration (**Hijrah**) to **Medina** in 622 CE marked the beginning of the Islamic calendar.  

## Achievements
- Established the first Muslim community (Ummah) in Medina.  
- Promoted equality, justice, and compassion.  
- Negotiated treaties, including the **Constitution of Medina**.  
- Led campaigns to defend the Muslim community.  
- Oversaw the peaceful conquest of Mecca in 630 CE.  

## Death
- Passed away in **632 CE** in Medina.  
- Buried in the Prophet’s Mosque (Masjid an‑Nabawi), Medina.  

## Legacy
- The **Qur’an**, revealed to him, is the central religious text of Islam.  
- His sayings and actions (**Hadith and Sunnah**) guide Muslim life.  
- Revered as the **Seal of the Prophets**.  
- His teachings shaped religion, law, culture, and civilization across the world.  

---

*Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) is remembered as a spiritual leader, reformer, and messenger whose life and teachings continue to inspire billions of Muslims worldwide.*
