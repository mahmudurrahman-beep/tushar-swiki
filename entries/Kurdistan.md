## Geography
- Area: ~392,000 km² (151,000 sq mi).  
- Located across the **northwestern Zagros Mountains** and **eastern Taurus Mountains**.  
- Borders: Turkey, Iran, Iraq, Syria.  
- Capital of Iraqi Kurdistan: **Erbil (Hawler)**.  

## People & Language
- Population: ~25–30 million Kurds.  
- Languages: Kurdish (Sorani, Kurmanji), plus Arabic, Persian, Turkish, Zaza–Gorani.  
- Religion: Majority Sunni Muslim, with minorities (Yazidis, Christians, Shia Kurds).  

## History
- Kurds trace their heritage back thousands of years in Mesopotamia.  
- After World War I, the **Treaty of Sèvres (1920)** proposed a Kurdish state, but it was never implemented.  
- Kurds faced repression in Turkey, Iran, Iraq, and Syria throughout the 20th century.  
- In Iraq, the **Anfal campaign (1980s)** under Saddam Hussein killed tens of thousands of Kurds.  
- Following the **1991 Gulf War**, a northern no-fly zone allowed Kurds to establish de facto autonomy.  

## Modern Autonomy
- The **Kurdistan Regional Government (KRG)** was formally recognized in 2005 under Iraq’s constitution.  
- The KRG controls Erbil, Duhok, and Sulaymaniyah provinces.  
- In 2017, the KRG held an **independence referendum**, but it was rejected by Iraq and neighboring states.  

## Current Issues
- Kurds continue to seek greater autonomy or independence.  
- Tensions exist with Turkey (due to PKK insurgency) and with Baghdad over oil revenues.  
- Syrian Kurds (Rojava) established autonomous administrations during the civil war.
