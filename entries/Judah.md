## Tribe of Judah
- Judah was the **fourth son of Jacob and Leah**.  
- The tribe of Judah settled in the southern part of ancient Israel, around Jerusalem and Hebron.  
- Became the most powerful tribe, producing leaders like **King David** and **King Solomon**.  
- The term **Jew** (Yehudi) comes from Judah, linking the tribe to Jewish identity.  

## Kingdom of Judah
- Existed from **c. 930 BCE – 587 BCE** after the division of the united monarchy of Israel.  
- Capital: **Jerusalem**.  
- Ruled by kings from the **Davidic dynasty** (Rehoboam was the first, Zedekiah the last).  
- Destroyed by the **Babylonians in 587 BCE**, leading to the exile.  

## Symbolism
- **Lion of Judah:** Represents strength, kingship, and messianic prophecy.  
- In Christianity, Jesus is often called the **“Lion of the Tribe of Judah”** (Revelation 5:5).  
- In Judaism, Judah symbolizes leadership and praise.  

## Legacy
- Judah gave rise to the Jewish people and identity.  
- The Davidic line is central to messianic expectations in both Judaism and Christianity.  
- The region of Judah became the foundation of Jewish culture and religion.
