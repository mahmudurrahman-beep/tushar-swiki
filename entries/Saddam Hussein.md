## Early Life
- Born: **28 April 1937**, near Tikrit, Iraq.  
- Came from a poor family; joined the Ba’ath Party in his youth.  
- Rose to prominence through political maneuvering and violence.  

## Rise to Power
- Played a key role in the **Ba’ath Party coup of 1968**.  
- Became President of Iraq in **1979**, succeeding Ahmed Hassan al-Bakr.  
- Consolidated power through purges, executions, and a strong security apparatus.  

## Rule (1979–2003)
- **Iran–Iraq War (1980–1988):** Prolonged conflict with massive casualties.  
- **Invasion of Kuwait (1990):** Triggered the **Gulf War (1991)**, leading to international sanctions.  
- Known for using **chemical weapons** against Kurdish civilians (Halabja, 1988).  
- Maintained a cult of personality and absolute control.  

## Downfall
- **2003:** U.S.-led coalition invaded Iraq, accusing Saddam of possessing WMDs.  
- **April 2003:** Baghdad fell; Saddam went into hiding.  
- **December 2003:** Captured near Tikrit in “Operation Red Dawn.”  

## Trial & Execution
- Tried by the Iraqi Special Tribunal for crimes against humanity.  
- Found guilty for the **Dujail massacre (1982)**.  
- Executed by hanging on **30 December 2006** in Baghdad.  

## Legacy
- Seen as both a **modernizer** (infrastructure, oil wealth) and a **dictator** responsible for mass killings.  
- His fall destabilized Iraq, leading to sectarian conflict and the rise of extremist groups.
