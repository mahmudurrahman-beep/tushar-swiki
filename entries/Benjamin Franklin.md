## Early Life
- Born: **January 17, 1706**, Boston, Massachusetts.  
- Tenth son of a soap and candle maker.  
- Apprenticed as a printer, later founded his own press in Philadelphia.  
- Published *Poor Richard’s Almanack*, filled with proverbs and practical wisdom.  

## Political Career
- Delegate to the **Continental Congress**.  
- Helped draft the **Declaration of Independence (1776)**.  
- Served as **U.S. ambassador to France**, securing crucial support during the Revolution.  
- Signed the **Treaty of Paris (1783)**, ending the war.  
- Contributed to the **U.S. Constitution (1787)**.  

## Scientific & Inventive Achievements
- **Lightning rod** – protected buildings from strikes.  
- **Bifocal glasses** – improved vision for near and far distances.  
- **Franklin stove** – more efficient heating system.  
- Conducted famous **kite experiment (1752)** proving electricity in lightning.  

## Legacy
- Known as “The First American” for his role in shaping national identity.  
- Advocated for **public libraries, education, and civic institutions**.  
- His wit, wisdom, and scientific curiosity made him a symbol of Enlightenment ideals.
