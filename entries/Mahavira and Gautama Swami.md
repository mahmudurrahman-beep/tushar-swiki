## Mahavira (599–527 BCE)
- **Identity**: The 24th and last *Tirthankara* of Jainism.  
- **Birth**: Born in *Kshatriyakundagrama* (near Vaishali, Bihar, India).  
- **Life**: Renounced worldly life at age 30, practiced severe asceticism for 12 years.  
- **Enlightenment**: Attained *Kevala Jnana* (omniscience) and preached non‑violence (*Ahimsa*), truth, and ascetic discipline.  
- **Legacy**: Reorganized Jain monastic community, emphasized equality and compassion.  
- **Death**: Attained *Nirvana* at *Pavapuri* in 527 BCE.  

## Gautama Swami (Indrabhuti Gautama)
- **Identity**: Chief disciple (*Ganadhara*) of Mahavira.  
- **Birth**: Born in *Magadha* to Vasubhūti and Prithvī.  
- **Role**: Senior‑most of Mahavira’s 11 chief disciples.  
- **Contribution**: Spread Mahavira’s teachings, headed the Jain community after Mahavira’s *Nirvana*.  
- **Spiritual Attainment**: Achieved *Kevala Jnana* (omniscience) himself.  
- **Succession**: After Gautama Swami’s death, leadership passed to **Sudharma Swami**.  

## Key Differences
| Aspect              | Mahavira                          | Gautama Swami                     |
|---------------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Role                | 24th Tirthankara (founder figure) | Chief disciple (*Ganadhara*)      |
| Birthplace          | Kshatriyakundagrama (Bihar)       | Gorbara, Magadha                  |
| Contribution        | Reformed Jainism, preached Ahimsa | Spread teachings, led after 527 BCE |
| Death/Nirvana       | 527 BCE, Pavapuri                 | Attained Nirvana later, age 92    |

---

*Mahavira is revered as the great teacher of Jainism, while Gautama Swami is honored as his foremost disciple who ensured the continuity of the faith after Mahavira’s passing.*
