## Origins
- Founded in **1994** in Kandahar by **Mullah Mohammad Omar** and other Afghan mujahideen.  
- Emerged during the Afghan civil war after the Soviet withdrawal.  
- Promised to restore order and enforce strict Sharia law.  

## First Rule (1996–2001)
- Captured Kabul in 1996, declaring the **Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan**.  
- Imposed harsh restrictions, especially on women and minorities.  
- Hosted **Osama bin Laden** and al-Qaeda, leading to the **U.S. invasion in 2001** after 9/11.  

## Insurgency (2001–2021)
- Regrouped after being ousted, waging a guerrilla war against U.S. and NATO forces.  
- Expanded influence in rural areas, exploiting corruption in the Afghan government.  
- Negotiated with the U.S. in Doha, Qatar, leading to troop withdrawal agreements.  

## Return to Power (2021–present)
- Seized Kabul in **August 2021** as U.S. forces withdrew.  
- Declared the **Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan** again.  
- Current leader: **Hibatullah Akhundzada** (Supreme Leader).  
- Policies include banning most education and work for women, restricting media, and enforcing strict dress codes.
