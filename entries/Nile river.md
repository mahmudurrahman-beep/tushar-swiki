## Geography
- **Length:** ~7,088 km (4,404 miles)  
- **Countries:** Egypt, Sudan, South Sudan, Ethiopia, Uganda, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, Eritrea[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1&citationId=2 "britannica.com")  
- **Sources:**  
  - **White Nile** – originates near Lake Victoria (Uganda, Tanzania, Kenya).  
  - **Blue Nile** – originates at Lake Tana in Ethiopia.  
- **Mouth:** Mediterranean Sea, forming the **Nile Delta** in Egypt.  
- **Basin size:** ~3.35 million km².  

## Historical Importance
- **Ancient Egypt:** The Nile’s annual floods deposited fertile silt, enabling agriculture and supporting one of the world’s earliest civilizations.  
- **Religion & Culture:** Egyptians worshipped the Nile as a divine gift.  
- **Trade & Transport:** Served as a highway for goods, people, and armies.  

## Modern Importance
- Provides **freshwater** for millions in northeast Africa.  
- Supports **agriculture, fishing, and hydroelectric power** (e.g., Aswan High Dam).  
- Critical for **regional cooperation and conflict** over water rights.  

## Challenges
- **Population growth** and **climate change** strain resources.  
- **Dams and irrigation projects** (like Ethiopia’s Grand Renaissance Dam) spark disputes among Nile Basin countries.  
- Pollution and overuse threaten ecosystems.  

## Legacy
The Nile remains a **symbol of life and continuity**, shaping cultures from ancient times to today. It is both a natural wonder and a geopolitical focal point in Africa.
