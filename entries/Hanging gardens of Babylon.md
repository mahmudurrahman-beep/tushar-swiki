## Description
- A series of **tiered terraces** resembling a green mountain.  
- Contained exotic plants, trees, and flowers.  
- Advanced irrigation systems supposedly lifted water from the Euphrates River.  

## Historical Accounts
- Ancient Greek writers like Strabo and Philo described the gardens.  
- Said to be built by **Nebuchadnezzar II** to please his wife, who missed the green hills of her homeland.  
- Some scholars argue they may have been located in **Nineveh**, not Babylon.  

## Mystery of Existence
- Unlike other wonders, **no Babylonian texts or ruins confirm the gardens**.  
- Archaeologists debate whether they were real, misattributed, or purely mythical.  
- If real, they showcased **extraordinary engineering** for their time.  

## Legacy
- Symbol of human creativity and ambition.  
- Continues to inspire art, literature, and modern architecture.  
- Represents the blend of **myth and history** in ancient civilizations.
