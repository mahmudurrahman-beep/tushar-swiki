## Early Life
- Born: **5 May 1818**, Trier, Prussia.  
- Studied philosophy and law at the **University of Bonn** and **University of Berlin**.  
- Influenced by German philosophy (Hegel) and French socialism.  

## Major Works
- **The Communist Manifesto (1848):** Co‑authored with Friedrich Engels, calling for workers of the world to unite.  
- **Das Kapital (1867–1883):** Analysis of capitalism, exploitation, and economic systems.  

## Key Ideas
- **Historical Materialism:** History driven by class struggle between oppressors (bourgeoisie) and oppressed (proletariat).  
- **Alienation:** Workers become estranged from their labor under capitalism.  
- **Class Conflict:** Capitalism inevitably leads to revolution and socialism.  
- **Communism:** A classless, stateless society as the ultimate stage of human development.  

## Political Activity
- Active in revolutionary movements across Europe.  
- Founded the **International Workingmen’s Association (First International)** in 1864.  
- Spent much of his life in exile, mainly in London.  

## Death & Legacy
- Died: **14 March 1883**, London, England.  
- Buried in **Highgate Cemetery**, London.  
- His theories inspired socialist and communist movements globally, shaping the 20th century.  
- Controversial legacy: admired as a critic of capitalism, criticized for authoritarian interpretations of Marxism.
