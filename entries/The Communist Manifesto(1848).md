## Background
- Written: **Late 1847**, published **21 February 1848** in London.  
- Authors: **Karl Marx** and **Friedrich Engels**.  
- Commissioned by the **Communist League** as a programmatic statement.  

## Structure
1. **Bourgeois and Proletarians** – Explains class struggle and capitalism’s rise.  
2. **Proletarians and Communists** – Defines communist goals and differences from other socialist movements.  
3. **Socialist and Communist Literature** – Critiques rival socialist ideologies.  
4. **Position of the Communists in Relation to the Various Existing Opposition Parties** – Calls for unity among workers.  

## Key Ideas
- **Class struggle drives history.**  
- Capitalism creates wealth but also exploitation.  
- The **proletariat will rise** against the bourgeoisie.  
- Abolition of private property in the means of production.  
- Establishment of a **classless society**.  

## Famous Quote
*"Workers of the world, unite! You have nothing to lose but your chains!"*  

## Legacy
- Inspired revolutions and socialist movements worldwide.  
- Still studied as a foundational text of **Marxism**.  
- Its critique of capitalism remains relevant in modern debates.
