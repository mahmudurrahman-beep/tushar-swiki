Alexander III of Macedon (356–323 BCE), known as Alexander the Great, was one of history’s most successful military commanders, creating one of the largest empires of the ancient world.

## Life and Background
- Born in Pella, Macedonia.  
- Tutored by Aristotle.  
- Became king of Macedonia in 336 BCE after the assassination of his father, Philip II.  

## Military Campaigns
1. **Conquest of Persia** – Defeated Darius III and captured the Persian Empire.  
2. **Expansion to India** – Reached the Indus Valley before his army halted.  
3. **Founding of Cities** – Established many cities named Alexandria.  

## Legacy
- Spread of Hellenistic culture across Asia, Africa, and Europe.  
- Fusion of Greek and Eastern traditions.  
- Died in Babylon in 323 BCE at age 32.
