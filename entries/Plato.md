Plato (427–347 BCE) was a student of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle. He founded the Academy in Athens, one of the earliest institutions of higher learning.

## Life and Background
- Born into an aristocratic family in Athens.  
- Witnessed Socrates’ trial and execution, which shaped his philosophy.  
- Founded the Academy around 387 BCE.  

## Philosophy
1. **Theory of Forms** – Belief in eternal, perfect realities beyond the material world.  
2. **Political Philosophy** – Advocated rule by philosopher‑kings in *The Republic*.  
3. **Epistemology** – Distinguished between opinion and true knowledge.  

## Works
- *The Republic*  
- *Phaedo*  
- *Symposium*  
- *Timaeus*
