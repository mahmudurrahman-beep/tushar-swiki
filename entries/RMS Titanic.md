## Definition
The **RMS Titanic** was a British passenger liner operated by the White Star Line. It was the largest ship afloat at the time of its maiden voyage in **April 1912** and is infamous for sinking after striking an iceberg.

## Construction
- Built at **Harland and Wolff shipyard** in Belfast, Northern Ireland.  
- Length: **269 meters (882 feet)**.  
- Tonnage: ~**46,000 gross tons**.  
- Designed to be the most luxurious ship of its era, with advanced safety features (though insufficient lifeboats).  

## Maiden Voyage
- Departed **Southampton, England** on **April 10, 1912**, bound for New York City.  
- Carried ~**2,224 passengers and crew**.  
- Notable passengers included wealthy elites, immigrants, and crew members.  

## Disaster
- On **April 14, 1912**, at 11:40 p.m., Titanic struck an iceberg in the North Atlantic.  
- Sank in the early hours of **April 15, 1912**.  
- Casualties: ~**1,500 people died**, ~**710 survived**.  
- One of the deadliest peacetime maritime disasters in history.  

## Aftermath
- Led to major reforms in maritime safety, including the **International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea (SOLAS)** in 1914.  
- Lifeboat requirements and iceberg monitoring were strengthened.
