## Definition
**Salvador Allende Gossens** was a Chilean physician and politician who became the **first Marxist to be democratically elected president in Latin America**. He served as President of Chile from **1970 until 1973**, when he was overthrown in a military coup.

## Early Life
- Born: **June 26, 1908**, in Valparaíso, Chile.  
- Profession: Trained as a physician, specialized in public health.  
- Political career: Co‑founder of Chile’s Socialist Party, long‑time senator before becoming president.  

## Presidency (1970–1973)
- Elected in **1970** as candidate of the **Popular Unity coalition**.  
- Promoted socialist reforms:  
  - Nationalization of copper mines and other industries.  
  - Agrarian reform (redistribution of land).  
  - Expansion of education and healthcare.  
- Faced economic challenges, strikes, and political polarization.  
- Relations with the U.S. deteriorated; the Nixon administration opposed his government.  

## Coup and Death
- On **September 11, 1973**, a military coup led by **General Augusto Pinochet** toppled Allende’s government.  
- Allende died inside the presidential palace (*La Moneda*) during the coup.  
- His death is officially recorded as suicide, though it remains a subject of debate and symbolism.  

## Legacy
- Seen by supporters as a martyr for democracy and socialism.  
- Critics argue his policies destabilized Chile’s economy and politics.  
- His overthrow marked the beginning of Pinochet’s military dictatorship (1973–1990).  
- Allende remains a powerful symbol of resistance, social justice, and democratic socialism worldwide.  

---

*Salvador Allende’s life and tragic death continue to resonate as a turning point in Chilean and Latin American history.*
