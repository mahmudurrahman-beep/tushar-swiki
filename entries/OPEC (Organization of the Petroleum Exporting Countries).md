## Definition
OPEC is an alliance of major oil‑producing countries that works to stabilize oil markets, secure fair prices, and ensure steady supply to consumers.

## Origins
- Founded: **September 14, 1960**, in Baghdad, Iraq.  
- Founding members: **Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Venezuela**.  
- Headquarters moved to **Vienna, Austria** in 1965.  

## Membership
- Current members: 12 countries (including Saudi Arabia, Iran, Iraq, UAE, Nigeria, Algeria, Libya, Angola, Venezuela, and others).  
- OPEC+ includes additional allies like **Russia** and other non‑OPEC producers.  

## Role in Global Oil Market
- Coordinates oil production levels among members.  
- Influences global oil prices by adjusting supply.  
- Accounts for around **38% of global oil production**.  
- Publishes the **World Oil Outlook (WOO)** annually, forecasting supply and demand.  

## Recent Developments
- OPEC+ currently produces about **43 million barrels per day** of crude oil.  
- Forecasts show supply and demand will remain closely balanced through **2026**, avoiding a glut.  

## Importance
- Provides stability in global energy markets.  
- Plays a central role in negotiations on energy security and climate policy.  
- Often criticized for manipulating oil prices, but also credited with preventing extreme volatility.
