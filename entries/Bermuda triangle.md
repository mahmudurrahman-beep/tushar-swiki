## Location
- Roughly spans between **Miami (Florida)**, **San Juan (Puerto Rico)**, and **Bermuda**.  
- Covers an area of about **500,000 square miles** of ocean.  

## Mysteries & Legends
- Popularized in the mid-20th century as a zone of unexplained disappearances.  
- Famous cases include:
  - **Flight 19 (1945):** A squadron of U.S. Navy bombers vanished during a training mission.  
  - **USS Cyclops (1918):** A U.S. Navy ship disappeared with 309 crew members.  
- Legends involve theories of **Atlantis, alien abductions, time warps, and magnetic anomalies**.  

## Scientific Explanations
- **Extreme weather:** Hurricanes and sudden storms are common in the region.  
- **Strong currents:** The Gulf Stream can quickly carry wreckage away.  
- **Human error:** Heavy air and sea traffic increases accident likelihood.  
- **Methane gas eruptions:** Possible destabilization of ships due to gas bubbles.  
- **Magnetic variations:** Can confuse navigation instruments.  

## Modern View
- The U.S. government and scientific organizations report **no evidence of unusual activity**.  
- Most disappearances are explained by **natural phenomena and human mistakes**.  
- The Bermuda Triangle remains more of a **myth and cultural fascination** than a scientific mystery.
