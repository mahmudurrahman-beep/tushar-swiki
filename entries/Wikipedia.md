Wikipedia is a free, web‑based, collaborative encyclopedia project supported by the non‑profit Wikimedia Foundation. It is written and maintained by volunteers around the world and is one of the most visited websites globally.

## Origins and History

- **Founded**: January 15, 2001  
- **Founders**: Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger  
- Initially launched as a complementary project to Nupedia, a more traditional expert‑written encyclopedia.  
- Quickly grew due to its open editing model.  

## Features

- **Open Editing** – Anyone with internet access can edit most articles.  
- **Neutral Point of View (NPOV)** – Articles must be written without bias.  
- **Free Content** – Licensed under Creative Commons Attribution‑ShareAlike.  
- **Multilingual** – Available in over 300 languages.  
- **References and Citations** – Reliability depends on verifiable sources.  

## Growth and Reach

- Millions of articles across languages.  
- One of the top ten most visited websites worldwide.  
- Used by students, researchers, and the general public for quick reference.  

## Governance

- Operated by the **Wikimedia Foundation**, headquartered in San Francisco, USA.  
- Community of editors and administrators enforce guidelines.  
- Policies include verifiability, notability, and civility.  

## Criticisms and Challenges

- Concerns about accuracy and vandalism.  
- Systemic bias due to editor demographics.  
- Reliance on volunteer contributions can lead to uneven coverage.  

## Importance

* Democratized access to knowledge.  
* Serves as a starting point for research.  
* Encourages collaborative knowledge creation.  
* Symbol of the open‑source and free culture movement.  

---

*Wikipedia remains a living, evolving encyclopedia, reflecting both the strengths and challenges of collaborative knowledge in the digital age.*
