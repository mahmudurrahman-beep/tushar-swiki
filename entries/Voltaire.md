## Life
- Born: **21 November 1694**, Paris, France.  
- Died: **30 May 1778**, Paris.  
- Pen name: Voltaire; real name: François-Marie Arouet.  
- Education: Collège Louis-le-Grand.  
- Resting place: Panthéon, Paris.  

## Works
- **Candide (1759):** Satirical novella criticizing optimism and injustice.  
- **Letters on the English (1733):** Praised English freedoms and criticized French institutions.  
- **The Age of Louis XIV (1751):** Historical analysis of French monarchy.  
- Plays, poems, essays, and thousands of letters.  

## Philosophy
- Central figure of the **Age of Enlightenment**.  
- Advocated **deism** (belief in a rational God, rejection of organized religion).  
- Championed **civil liberties**: freedom of speech, freedom of religion, fair trials.  
- Criticized intolerance, superstition, and absolute monarchy.  

## Legacy
- Voltaire’s ideas influenced the **French Revolution** and modern democratic thought.  
- Famous quote: *“I disapprove of what you say, but I will defend to the death your right to say it.”* (attributed, though not directly written by him).  
- Remembered as a **fearless critic of authority** and defender of human rights.
