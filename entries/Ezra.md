## Identity
- Name: Ezra (Hebrew: עֶזְרָא, meaning “help” or “Yah helps”).  
- Role: **Priest (kohen)** and **scribe (sofer)**.  
- Period: Flourished in the **5th–4th century BCE**.  
- Associated with the **Books of Ezra and Nehemiah** in the Hebrew Bible.  

## Historical Context
- After the **Babylonian exile**, Jews were allowed to return to Jerusalem under Persian rule.  
- Ezra arrived in Jerusalem around **458 BCE**, during the reign of **Artaxerxes I of Persia**[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.biblestudytools.com/bible-study/topical-studies/who-was-ezra-and-why-is-his-book-significant.html?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=2 "Bible Study Tools").  
- His mission: restore Jewish law and worship in the rebuilt Temple.  

## Contributions
- **Law of Moses:** Ezra emphasized strict observance of the Torah.  
- **Religious Reform:** Opposed intermarriage with foreign peoples to preserve Jewish identity.  
- **Teaching:** Devoted himself to studying, practicing, and teaching God’s law[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.biblestudytools.com/bible-study/topical-studies/who-was-ezra-and-why-is-his-book-significant.html?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=2 "Bible Study Tools").  
- **Scripture:** Traditionally credited with editing or compiling parts of the Hebrew Bible, including **Chronicles** and possibly **Malachi**[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ezra?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "Wikipedia").  

## Legacy
- Honored in **Judaism, Christianity, and Islam** as a reformer and teacher.  
- Feast days: **July 13 (Catholic Church)**; commemorated in Eastern Orthodoxy[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ezra?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "Wikipedia").  
- Seen as a model of **faithful leadership**, ensuring the survival of Jewish tradition after exile.
