## Early Life
- Born: **July 28, 1954**, in Sabaneta, Barinas, Venezuela.  
- Background: Came from a modest family; trained as a military officer.  
- Founded the **Bolivarian Revolutionary Movement‑200 (MBR‑200)** in the 1980s.  

## Rise to Power
- Led a failed coup attempt in **1992** against President Carlos Andrés Pérez.  
- Gained popularity as a populist leader.  
- Elected President in **1998**, taking office in 1999.  

## Presidency
- Introduced the **Bolivarian Revolution**, inspired by Simón Bolívar’s ideals.  
- Promoted **socialist policies**, nationalized key industries (oil, telecommunications, electricity).  
- Launched social programs (*misiones*) to reduce poverty, improve healthcare, and expand education.  
- Drafted a new constitution in **1999**, renaming the country the **Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela**.  

## International Role
- Strongly opposed U.S. foreign policy, especially in Latin America.  
- Allied with countries like Cuba, Bolivia, and Iran.  
- Promoted regional integration through initiatives like **ALBA (Bolivarian Alliance for the Americas)**.  

## Legacy
- Supporters praise him for reducing poverty and empowering marginalized communities.  
- Critics accuse him of authoritarianism, weakening democratic institutions, and mismanaging the economy.  
- Died: **March 5, 2013**, in Caracas, Venezuela, after battling cancer.  
- Succeeded by **Nicolás Maduro**.  

---

*Hugo Chávez remains a polarizing figure: celebrated as a champion of the poor and condemned as an authoritarian who left Venezuela deeply divided.*
