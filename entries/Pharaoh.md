## Role & Authority
- Supreme ruler of ancient Egypt, combining **religious, political, and military power**.  
- Believed to be the **living embodiment of Horus** (the falcon god) and later associated with **Ra** (the sun god).  
- Tasked with maintaining **ma’at** (order and balance in the universe).  

## Symbols of Power
- **Crook and flail:** Represented kingship and fertility.  
- **Double crown (Pschent):** Symbolized rule over Upper and Lower Egypt.  
- **False beard:** Sign of divine kingship.  
- **Cartouche:** Oval hieroglyph enclosing the pharaoh’s name.  

## Famous Pharaohs
- **Narmer (c. 3100 BCE):** Unified Upper and Lower Egypt.  
- **Khufu (c. 2600 BCE):** Built the Great Pyramid of Giza.  
- **Hatshepsut (c. 1479–1458 BCE):** Powerful female pharaoh, expanded trade.  
- **Akhenaten (c. 1353–1336 BCE):** Introduced monotheism (worship of Aten).  
- **Tutankhamun (c. 1332–1323 BCE):** Famous for his intact tomb discovered in 1922.  
- **Ramses II (c. 1279–1213 BCE):** Known as “Ramses the Great,” expanded Egypt’s empire.  

## Legacy
- Pharaohs left behind monumental architecture: **pyramids, temples, and statues**.  
- Their reigns shaped Egyptian religion, art, and culture for over **3,000 years**.  
- The concept of divine kingship influenced later civilizations.
